Three Shapes Vs. CAGED

There is a guitar learning system known as CAGED that has similarities to Echo Alpha Delta. CAGED opened the door to visualising chord shapes across the fretboard, and many players have learned from it. We believe there are a number of reasons why this system is an improvement, for all skill levels.

The Naming Problem

CAGED has a fundamental limitation that we have to address. The chord shapes are still named after the open-position pitch they originated from. When a player puts on a capo or forms a barre chord and uses one of those shapes, the pitch name of the shape no longer matches the pitch being played. Play a C shape at the 4th fret and you're voicing an E chord, but CAGED still calls it the C shape. The shape name and the pitch name collide. This is the central reason we created Echo Alpha Delta: to decouple these crucial shapes from pitch names, so they can be put to use more intuitively.

Redundancy

Take a look at an open C chord and you will see a Delta shape sitting at the top of it. An open G chord has an Alpha shape in the heart of the chord and an Echo shape at both the top and bottom. Two of the five CAGED shapes are composites that already contain our three shapes within them. Echo Alpha Delta is not missing anything; it is distilled.

Three Shapes, Not Five

As each shape expands to hold every chord quality and every scale, the learning load multiplies. Three shapes is already a significant commitment. Five is unnecessarily steep, especially when two of the five are redundant. That is 60% more information for no additional coverage of the fretboard.

Memory

Beyond giving the shapes new names, we give each one a distinct character and a colour: Echo is blue, Alpha is red, Delta is green. These are not decorative. They are deliberate memory aids, designed so the player can internalise the map away from the instrument and project the three shapes onto the fretboard at all times. If the player can get a memory foothold with this map, they can use it as a platform to play better.
