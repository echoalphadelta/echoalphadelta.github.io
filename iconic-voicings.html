Iconic Voicings

Open chords, barre chords, and essential shapes — sorted by their home shape

Every chord you will ever play on a guitar is a variant of one of the three shapes. The voicings below are not merely useful — they are the common language of the instrument. Knowing which shape each one comes from is the first step to being able to move it anywhere on the neck.

Echo Shape — Open E Form

The open E and Em are the foundation of the Echo shape. The root sits on the low E string at the nut. Barre across any fret and the entire shape shifts to a new key.

Alpha Shape — Open A Form

The open A form has its root on the A string at the nut. In the Alpha barre chord, the index finger covers the nut position and the shape shifts up the neck to any key.

Delta Shape — Open D Form

The Delta shape is the smallest of the three, spanning four strings. The root lives on the D string at the nut. Compact but complete, it covers enough of the chord to be unmistakable anywhere on the neck.

Beyond Open Position

These voicings draw from across all three shapes. Open G and open C each contain elements of multiple shapes combined. Power chords strip a shape down to its two strongest tones. Suspended chords replace the 3rd with the 2nd or 4th, creating a sound that belongs to no single quality.
